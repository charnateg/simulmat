# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo builder / creative technologist experimenting in personal time. No fixed audience — the simulator is a playground for exploring cloth physics, material behaviour, and interactive tools. The "user" is effectively the author.

## Product Purpose

Real-time cloth physics simulator built on Three.js. Lets the user configure material type, physics parameters, and lighting, then interact with the cloth using tools (hand, knife, fire, push, bullet). Success means the simulation feels tactile, responsive, and fun to break.

## Brand Personality

Precise, obsessive, electric. Feels like a piece of internal tooling from a sci-fi R&D lab that was never meant to look pretty but ended up looking great anyway.

## Anti-references

- Generic dark SaaS dashboards (Vercel clone aesthetic)
- "Futuristic UI" templates with flat glowing cards and no depth
- Anything that looks like it came from a Three.js starter kit

## Design Principles

1. **Earned glow** — lighting effects only where they earn their place. Not decorative; structural.
2. **Hierarchy over decoration** — every visual element should reinforce what matters now (active tool, live values, physics state), not just look cool.
3. **Dense but breathable** — high information density in the panel, but with enough rhythm that the eye can navigate fast.
4. **System coherence** — controls should feel like they belong to the same machine. Consistent language across labels, values, buttons.
5. **No tourist aesthetics** — every choice should feel like it could only belong to this tool, not borrowed from a mood board.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target (personal project). Minimum bar: body text readable at comfortable viewing distance, interactive elements clearly distinguishable.
